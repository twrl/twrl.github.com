httpRange-14 and the Dark Resource

In web parlance, a resource is any identifiable thing. A resource need not have a digital representation, and the representation of a resource may also be a distinct resource in it’s own right. There is a whole gamut of issues about resources, possibly the best known is httpRange-14.

A lot of these questions only become important when you start getting involved with Semantic Web technology. For example, is this properly described as a web page or as a blog post?

I hold to the convention that http://twrl.github.com/2012/08/httprange-14-and-the-dark-resource/ is distinct from http://twrl.github.com/2012/08/httprange-14-and-the-dark-resource/index.html. They’re related, very much so, but distinct. In particular

@base <http://twrl.github.com/>
@prefix schema: <http://schema.org/>
@prefix dc: <http://purl.org/dc/terms/>

</2012/08/httprange-14-and-the-dark-resource> a schema:BlogPost;
	dc:hasFormat </2012/08/httprange-14-and-the-dark-resource/index.html>.

</2012/08/httprange-14-and-the-dark-resource/index.html> a schema:WebPage.

From this point of view, there are a lot of “missing” resources out there - ones which are not described. Taking the view that the abstract resource and it’s representation are distinct, people mostly seem to describe only one of the two.

But why does this matter, you may say. Well, what a person wants to access is the abstract, logical resource. But what they need is it’s representation, and given the variety of media through which we can access the web - smartphones, tablets, TVs, etc - they need the right representation. Having the correct semantics makes it possible to identify the best of many possible representations, as well as allowing better indexes.
