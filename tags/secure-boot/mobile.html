  • The FSF have my goat!

    26 September 2012

    I’ve never made a secret of my mixed feelings about the FSF and what they do. For anyone who isn’t aware, the UEFI forum have devised a standard called Secure Boot. Secure Boot requires the firmware to cryptographically verify operating system images at load time, using public keys stored in NVRAM.
